Overview

Purpose

I want this website to showcase the interests and skill that I have as one who wants to get involved with game development. Ideally, I want this website to act as a hub for all my projects. Both finished and in progress.

Audience

I want this website to target current and prospective employers. To become a website where future employers or people who have same interests can see what I have and are currently working on, and to see the skills that I can display.

Content

Home page

When I first started designing this website, a juggled with a lot of ideas on what this could be. I wanted it to be good, I wanted to find use out of it after I finished the class. And most importantly I wanted this to matter. What do I want to do with this. This phrase tossed around in my head every night for a week. I needed something interesting I could latch onto to make this more then just a class assignment. I needed a reason. And it occurred to me, what if I were to use this to showcase my projects? Looking at the idea of using a website to showcase my work doesn’t sound too bad. Artist do it. Or at least there are websites that artists can post their work. Why can’t I do the same? I’m a software engineer what better way to showcase my projects then through a website? I want to show my skill, my ingenuity, my creativity. But what does this Showcase have in it? What kind of projects. All of them. How? Videos, photos, in-progress updates, etc. It doesn’t matter what this website showcases except that it showcases what I have and am working on. It becomes an online resume to direct employers too.

Images for the Home page

Showcasing

Here Is where I will show the majority of my finished and work-in-progress projects. Ideally I would love to have in depth reviews of each and every project. But I believe having a multitude of items with a few photos and a video to preview the project and then include a hyperlink to video going in depth. Now what kind of activities would I want visitors to see and interact with? Reviewable materials. Photos, videos, and possibly downloadable content in stable form to test out what I have been working on if it’s a project like a video game. I want to come to this website because I want them to get an idea of how I do things. For employers I was them to see what I’ve done. For regular everyday visitors I want them to see possibilities. Perhaps they would feel inspired to follow my work or even pass around my work to others with the same interests. This is also an excellent chance to use a website as a networking tool. Having a link attached to resumes, LinkedIn, and other professional websites can bring in more people who would have an interest in what you do.

Images for the Page 2

Who, What, and Why

The Who, What, and Why. I’ve slowly been covering these basic questions on other pages, but this is also going to act as a FAQ board. Giving others the chance to leave private comments to criticize my work. Whether its constructive or not. I want to be able to answer as many questions as possible that visitors will have. Who am I? My name is Ashur Ellsworth, a prospective software engineer with an interest in game development. What is this website? This website showcases my projects that I’ve completed and those that I am currently working on. Why? Why in this format? I want what I’m doing freely available to everyone in the most accessible way. Anyone with a working internet connection can and should be able to view my work. These are some of the more basic FAQs I’ll add, and I hope to add a lot more as I work on this website and even more so after I finish this class. But what happens next? The website is called IndomitableGames. I want to eventually use this website I’ve created as a steppingstone to starting my own game development company.
